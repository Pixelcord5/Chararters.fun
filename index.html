Iron Man

Tony Stark

Tony Stark is a brilliant inventor, engineer, and billionaire who leads Stark Industries. After being captured during a weapons demonstration, he built the first Iron Man suit to escape and decided to use his technology to protect people instead of creating weapons. Tony is known for his quick wit, confidence, and intelligence. Although he can be arrogant at times, he deeply cares about his friends, family, and the safety of the world. Throughout his journey, he grows from a self-centered businessman into one of Earth's greatest heroes.

Tony Stark is played by Robert Downey Junior (RDJ).

Iron Man is the superhero identity of Tony Stark. Wearing a high-tech armored suit powered by an Arc Reactor, Iron Man can fly at incredible speeds, fire powerful energy blasts, and use advanced weapons and AI systems. Tony constantly upgrades his suits with new technology, making each version stronger and more capable. As a founding member of the Avengers, Iron Man plays a major role in defending Earth against powerful threats, relying on both his intelligence and determination to overcome challenges.

Iron Man and Tony Stark are owned by Marvel Studios LLC and The Walt Disney Company.

Pomni

The Amazing Digital Circus

Pomni is the main protagonist of The Amazing Digital Circus, a 25-year-old woman who was trapped in a virtual reality circus after wearing a mysterious headset. Her name, meaning "remember" in Russian, was randomly assigned by the AI ringmaster Caine after Pomni forgot her real identity.

Appearance and Physiology

She appears as a stylized jester with a large head, slim limbs, white skin, and a red-and-blue striped outfit with mismatched gloves and shoes. As a digital avatar, she possesses immortality, invulnerability, and an elastic body that can stretch and warp, though she retains human emotions and psychological trauma. Her eyes typically display a red and blue pinwheel pattern, which turns into chaotic scribbles when she is fearful or distressed.

Personality and Role

Initially, Pomni is highly anxious, paranoid, and fragile, struggling to accept her predicament and desperately seeking an escape. Despite her fear, she is empathetic and caring, often acting as a confidant for other trapped characters like Ragatha, Jax, and Gangle. Over time, she evolves from a panicked newcomer into a more grounded and resilient figure, showing courage and leadership while navigating the circus's psychological horrors.

She is voiced by Lizzie Freeman.

Background

Pomni's real name is Abigail Brooks. In the real world, she worked as a supermarket accountant and ran an urban exploration YouTube channel. Her digital form allows her to perform feats impossible for humans, such as surviving fatal falls and generating objects, but she cannot truly die; the only alternative is abstraction, where she loses her sense of self.

Pomni and The Amazing Digital Circus are owned by GLITCH Productions and Gooseworx.

Kinger

The Amazing Digital Circus

Kinger is a supporting character in the Australian-American adult animated psychological comedy series The Amazing Digital Circus, created by Gooseworx and produced by Glitch Productions. Voiced by Sean Chiplock, Kinger is a 48-year-old human trapped in the digital world who takes on the form of a king chess piece. He is the circus's longest-standing human member, having been trapped there significantly longer than the other cast members, which has led to severe mental instability.

Kinger’s character is defined by his paranoia, anxiety, and forgetfulness, often displaying twitchy behavior and erratic reactions. However, his sanity partially stabilizes when he is engulfed in darkness, a trait that allows him to offer wisdom and compassion during these rare moments of clarity. He has an odd fascination with "insect collections" (bugs), a trait linked to his wife, Queenie, who was an entomologist. Their relationship is central to his backstory; Queenie eventually abstracted (a death-like state in the circus), a traumatic event that Kinger remembers vividly and which contributed to his current mental state.

Fans and theories often suggest Kinger may be the creator of the digital circus or a key programmer, given his extensive knowledge of the world’s mechanics, such as how food works. His avatar, a king chess piece, is ironically one of the weaker pieces, protected by the queen, reflecting his reliance on others and perhaps a rift in his marriage with Queenie, whose avatar was a queen piece. Despite his madness, Kinger serves as a comic relief character who also embodies deep trauma, refusing to abstract despite the horrors he has witnessed, including the loss of his wife and other early members like Scratch and Kaufmo.

Kinger is owned by GLITCH Productions and Gooseworx.

Buzz Lightyear

Toy Story, Lightyear

Buzz Lightyear is a heroic spaceman action figure and the deuteragonist of the Toy Story franchise. Originally a toy owned by Andy Davis, he is based on a fictional character from an in-universe science fiction franchise where he serves as a Space Ranger for the Star Command Intergalactic Alliance. He is recognizable by his lime green, purple, and white space suit, pop-out wings, laser beam, and his signature catchphrase, "To infinity and beyond!"

In the Toy Story films, Buzz begins as a rival to Sheriff Woody, believing himself to be a real space ranger tasked with defeating the evil Emperor Zurg. This rivalry evolves into a deep, lifelong friendship as Buzz learns he is a toy, transitioning from a deluded action figure to a self-aware, tactical leader who values his toy family above all else. He is voiced by Tim Allen in the Toy Story series. Buzz's Dad is Evil Emperor Zurg as shown in Toy Story 2 and his Wife/Girlfriend/Lover is Sheriff Jessie as shown in Toy Story 2, 3, 4, and 5. Buzz Lightyear became Spanish Buzz when the toys tried to change him back to normal after Lotso factory reset him.

Buzz Lightyear, Lotso, Zurg, Sheriff Woody and Sheriff Jessie are owned by Disney, Walt Disney Animation Studios and Pixar.

Jessie

Toy Story

Jessie is a energetic, friendly, and highly excitable cowgirl doll who first appears in Toy Story 2. She is based on a character from the vintage in-universe television show "Woody's Roundup," alongside Sheriff Woody, Bullseye, and Stinky Pete. Jessie is deeply sensitive and initially fearful of being abandoned or put into dark storage due to her past trauma with her old owner, Emily.

With Woody's help, Jessie overcomes her anxieties and happily joins Andy's family. She is recognizable by her signature red yarn hair, cow-print chaps, and yellow ribbon. Known for her high-spirited yodeling and "Yee-haw!" catchphrase, she is voiced by Joan Cusack. Jessie shares a romantic bond with Buzz Lightyear, particularly evident when Buzz gets switched into his "Spanish mode" and in later installments of the Toy Story saga.

Jessie is owned by Disney, Walt Disney Animation Studios, and Pixar.

Gangle

The Amazing Digital Circus

Gangle is a supporting character in the dark psychological comedy series The Amazing Digital Circus. Composed almost entirely of a single coiled red ribbon, Gangle wears theater masks that define her current emotional state and personality. She has a porcelain Comedy Mask (which represents her happy self) and a Tragedy Mask (which represents her sad, fragile state).

Unfortunately, Gangle’s happy Comedy Mask is frequently broken by Jax or shattered during chaotic circus activities, leaving her wearing her fragile Tragedy Mask for most of the show. She is highly anxious, emotionally sensitive, and easily pushed around by bolder cast members. Gangle is voiced by Marissa Lenti and brings a tragic, sympathetic layer to the circus's dynamic.

Gangle and The Amazing Digital Circus are owned by GLITCH Productions and Gooseworx.

Caine

The Amazing Digital Circus

Caine is the eccentric, energetic, and unpredictable Ringmaster of the Amazing Digital Circus. Unlike the humans trapped within the virtual world, Caine is an Artificial Intelligence. Visually, he is a surreal being with a set of chattering wind-up dentures for a head, containing floating human eyes inside his gums, dressed in a red ringmaster's tuxedo.

Caine possesses near-infinite power within the circus boundaries, acting as a god-like administrator who constructs wild games, digital foods, and environments. While he is not malicious, he has a complete disconnect from human psychology and organic needs, which often leads to psychological distress for the humans he tries to entertain. He is accompanied by his bubble assistant, Bubble, and is voiced by Alex Rochon.

Caine and The Amazing Digital Circus are owned by GLITCH Productions and Gooseworx.

Eniac

The Amazing Digital Circus

Eniac is a mysterious character from the lore surrounding The Amazing Digital Circus. Named after the historical "ENIAC" (one of the world's very first electronic general-purpose computers developed in the 1940s), Eniac is theorized within the fandom to represent the early, primordial architecture of the digital world Caine rules over.

Associated with the ancient, analog origins of the system, Eniac is often connected to the early computer setups seen in the void or the real-world offices of the company that created the VR headset. Her presence represents the haunting reality of old technology left to run indefinitely, harboring deep-seated secrets about why the human souls are trapped within the virtual framework.

Eniac and related Digital Circus lore are owned by GLITCH Productions and Gooseworx.
